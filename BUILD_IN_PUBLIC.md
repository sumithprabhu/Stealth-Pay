## Day 1

Day 1 of #BuildOn0G

Stealth Pay is a ZK privacy layer for ERC-20 tokens on 0G Chain.

shield tokens into a pool. transact privately inside it. withdraw to any address.
no relayer. no trusted party. just math.

https://try-stealth-pay.vercel.app/

---

## Day 2

Day 2 of #BuildOn0G

the shield circuit is written in Noir.

you put in a spending key, token address, amount, and a random salt.
the circuit outputs one thing: a commitment hash.
nothing else is revealed. ever.

https://try-stealth-pay.vercel.app/

---

## Day 3

Day 3 of #BuildOn0G

every commitment goes into a depth-20 Poseidon2 Merkle tree.

supports up to ~1M notes. the root is what the spend circuit proves membership against.
the local SDK tree and the on-chain tree always match — same hash function, same zero chain.

https://try-stealth-pay.vercel.app/

---

## Day 4 

Day 4 of #BuildOn0G

Stealth Pay uses two separate keys.

wallet key: signs Ethereum transactions
spending key: generates ZK proofs, never leaves the client

your wallet being compromised does not expose your private notes.
the keys serve different purposes by design.

https://try-stealth-pay.vercel.app/

## Day 8

Day 8 of #BuildOn0G

the spend circuit proves five things in zero knowledge:

- you own the input notes
- they are in the current Merkle tree
- nullifiers are correctly derived
- output commitments are correctly formed
- input value equals output value

no amount, no sender, no recipient on-chain.

## Day 15

Day 15 of #BuildOn0G

the note hint system uses 0G Storage.

when A sends privately to B, the note is encrypted with ECIES and uploaded to 0G Storage.
B's next sync() call finds and decrypts it automatically.
no manual communication between sender and receiver.

https://try-stealth-pay.vercel.app/
